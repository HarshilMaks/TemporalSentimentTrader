### **1. The Strategy: "The Triangulation Method"**

To get "insider-level" accuracy, you only trade when **three independent data sources** agree. This filters out 90% of noise and leaves only high-probability setups.

#### **Layer 1: The "Insider" Data (Legal Insider Trading)**

This is the closest to what you asked for. You track the people who actually know the company's future.

* **Corporate Insiders (SEC Form 4):** When a CEO or CFO buys their own stock with their own money, the stock historically outperforms.
* *Data Source:* SEC EDGAR (via `sec-api` or `edgartools` library).


* **Senate/Congress Trading:** Tracking what US Senators trade (e.g., the "Nancy Pelosi Tracker"). They often trade on legislative knowledge.
* *Data Source:* `House Stock Watcher` API or `Senate Stock Watcher`.



#### **Layer 2: The "Dark" Flow (Institutional Money)**

Retail traders trade on "Lit Exchanges" (NASDAQ/NYSE). Institutions often trade in **Dark Pools** or use **Unusual Options** to hide their moves.

* **Volume Anomalies:** If a stock is flat but Volume is 300% higher than average, someone is accumulating silently.
* **Options Flow:** Heavy Call buying (especially "Out of the Money") implies someone expects a massive jump.

#### **Layer 3: The "Hype" (Your Current Project)**

* **Reddit/Social Sentiment:** This acts as the "match" that lights the fuelpile accumulated by Insiders and Institutions.

### **2. The Trading Algorithm: "Regime-Weighted Voting"**

Don't just use one model. Use a voting system where different signals have different "veto" powers.

**The Algorithm Flow:**

1. **Regime Detection (The Gatekeeper):**
* *Logic:* Is the S&P 500 (SPY) above its 200-day Moving Average?
* *Rule:* If Market is Bearish, **DISABLE ALL BUYS** (or switch to Short strategies).


2. **Score Generation (0-100):**
* `Insider_Score`: CEO Bought = +30 points.
* `Flow_Score`: Volume > 200% avg = +20 points.
* `Sentiment_Score`: Reddit Mentions spiking = +20 points.
* `Technical_Score`: RSI < 30 (Oversold) = +30 points.


3. **Execution Trigger:**
* **BUY Signal:** Total Score > 80.

### **3. System Architecture (Backend Design)**

To handle **Live Ticking** and volume analysis, the simple cron-job architecture isn't enough. You need an **Event-Driven Architecture**.

**Technology Stack Changes:**

* **FastAPI:** Keeps managing the API.
* **Redis Pub/Sub:** Handles the "Ticking" data (real-time).
* **TimescaleDB (Extension for PostgreSQL):** You MUST use this. Standard Postgres is too slow for storing millions of millisecond-level stock ticks.

#### **The "Live Ticking" Implementation**

To support "live volume" and real-time updates without crashing your server:

1. **The Ingestor (Worker):**
* Connects to a WebSocket (e.g., Alpaca/Polygon.io).
* Receives a trade: `{ticker: "AAPL", price: 150.01, volume: 100}`.
* **Action 1:** Pushes to Redis Channel `live_trades`.
* **Action 2:** Aggregates 1-minute candle -> Saves to TimescaleDB.


2. **The Analyzer (Worker):**
* Subscribes to Redis `live_trades`.
* **Real-time Logic:** `Current_Volume += incoming_volume`.
* *Check:* Is `Current_Volume` > `Average_Volume_at_this_time_of_day`?
* *Alert:* If yes, trigger "Unusual Volume" event.


3. **The Frontend Socket:**
* FastAPI exposes a WebSocket endpoint `/ws/stocks/{ticker}`.
* It listens to Redis and pushes updates to the user's React dashboard instantly.
